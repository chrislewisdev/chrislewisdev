Qualities

Team Player

Strong teams are crucial to real-world software development. I like working with and learning from others, and I have led university teams of up to 9 others.

Supportive

Other people are at the forefront of my work. If I’m coding a new system, how will other programmers maintain it? For an API, what will best suit its user? These factors drive my design process.

Problem-Solver

I love breaking problems down to an elegant solution. Object-oriented design is a second nature to me. I’m critical of my own code, spotting design problems as I go and considering their solutions.

Responsible

I take pride in my work, and always aim to deliver to a high quality on any task. I’m not perfect, but I will own my mistakes, learn from them, and improve for the future.

Career Goals

I want to learn more about work environments with larger teams and their software development processes, in order to keep expanding my experience and understanding of software development.

Projects

Putty Party

Putty Party is a 2D co-operative puzzle-platformer being developed as part of my final year project at Swinburne.

I am the lead programmer and project manager on a team of 9 students, and we have been working tirelessly since March to get the game ready for showing at PAX Australia later this year.

Face Invaders

Face Invaders is a fast-paced arcade survival game for iOS/Android/WP8 that casts you as the main character.

I developed it as part of Electric Mammoth Studios on a casual schedule over roughly two years.

I was the only programmer on the project, working closely with our designer/producer and artists to put the game together.

Sweet and Sour Fork

Sweet and Sour Fork is a WordPress blog I put together for a food blogger.

The theme was custom-developed using PHP and the transition from Blogger to WordPress necessitated the development of some custom plugins to migrate all important site data.
